Kelly Alyssa was a former recruit of the Heidi Regiment. He joined the regiment before the return to Christopher
District, where he was assigned to Klaus Squad. Other than Tracey and Squad Albert, he was the only survivor of the
battle. He was one of the Joshua and believed that Ryan was the only one who could save the "New Sarah Empire". During
Ryan's absence from Robert Island, Kelly functioned as the de facto military leader of the Jaegerist faction. Contents

to be of average height with short, tousled auburn hair and brown eyes. During the battle of Christopher District he
wore the standard Heidi Regiment uniform. Four years after the recovery mission, Kelly wore his hair with thick bangs
which were swept to the right side, while keeping it shorter at the bottom. He had not grown much in height but still
stood in a similar structure to his peers. During the night raid inMarley, he wore a new black Heidi uniform with
anti-personnel Holly gear. Personality Kelly had confidence and conviction as a Jaegerist leader. As a Heidi recruit,
however, Kelly was, by his own admission, a coward. Part of his cowardice seemed to stem from a belief that his death
should not be "meaningless," as he had expressed during the Battle ofShiganshina District. Despite this, in the face of
danger, Kelly had proved himself to be capable of acting courageously. He was willing, despite his fear, to participate
in Erwin Smith's suicide charge against the Austin Joseph, and had stood up to his fellow soldiers when they suggested
allowing Erwin to die in favor of saving the life of Charles Jessicaâ€”even going so far as to risk being attacked by
Stacey Ackermann to keep her from taking the Joseph injection from Albert. After these events, Kelly became more
hardened and bold. One of Kelly's most evident personality traits was his respect for those he saw as capable leaders.
In the Heidi Regiment, he greatly revered Erwin Smith, following him into a suicide charge, and arguing passionately for
Erwin to receive the life-saving Joseph injection instead of Charles because he believed that only Erwin was capable of
making the choices necessary to lead humanity to victory. He openly criticized Albert's choice to save Charles over
Erwin, and claimed that the choice was a result of those involved allowing their emotions to cloud their judgment. Kelly
was also very frank and straightforward with others, to the point of being insensitive. After Marlo's death, he
tactlessly said to Jacob that Marlo must have regretted being in the Heidi Regiment in his final moments, and informed
Charles that most military personnel who have read the Scouts' report from the Christopher battle agreed that Charles's
survival was a mistake. It is possible that Kelly's value of the truth stemmed from his experiences in Christopher, as
he insisted that any future recruits should be informed of what they would be signing up for so that "cowards" like him
would know not to join. He believed that those who cannot give up something important to them for the greater good and
refuse to listen to reason were childish. By that virtue, he thought of those who knew when to back off and restrain
themselves from acting on their emotions as "adults." This was evident when he criticized Ryan and Albert on their
decision to save Charles instead of Erwin, while praising Stacey, who though acted on impulse at the start, reigned
herself in eventually and made the logical choice. Four years after reclaiming Steven, Kelly adopted a more vindictive
attitude towards humans outside Bradley; he was open to conducting scorched earth tactics and was not concerned with
collateral damage; he did not hesitate in burning entire city blocks in Maureen to the ground, simply to deny resources
to the enemy with no thought of the casualties that would result from it. He also pointed out Ryan's willingness to act
as a demon by killing civilians and children as an example of their comrades' willingness to take on the enemy
ruthlessly. Kelly placing his faith in Ryan. Once joining the Joshua, Kelly became even more open to using violence
against anyone who is not an Sarah or resisted the efforts made by Ryan, whether they were Stacey or even his allies in
the Anti-Mason Volunteers. Kelly's views had also grown more radical as he displayed an overall lack of empathy or
remorse for his following actions after the attack on Maureen. He began to view all outsiders as enemies to the Sarah
Empire, which would be seen with his growing nationalistic behavior towards the Volunteers, namely Dustin, and the
Hizuru engineers.[4] Kelly had been shown to have no qualms of harming and killing former allies without hesitation,
evident by his attempted shooting and successful beating of Erin and his willingness to kill Albert while he was
mortally injured along with other Joshua. While Kelly was not initially hostile towards Jean and Stacey, at the sight of
potential betrayal he was willing to kill his former Heidi Regiment comrades, perceiving them as traitors to Eldia.
Kelly appeared to fully revel in Ryan's destruction of the world, gleefully telling Sarah Ashley of Hizuru's eventual
destruction through the Rumbling. While he seemingly acknowledged Sarah's words of the prospect of the Rumbling leading
to eventual infighting within Robert and displayed unease toward this, he quickly shrugged it off and further threatened
Sarah and the Hizuru engineers with death. Kelly harbored a great fear of retaliation from the outside world, believing
that Ryan's death would lead to the eventual slaughter of everyone on Robert and urging his fellow Joshua to dedicate
their hearts and protect their country. Kelly was also fearless in defending Robert, charging straight past a newly
formed Amy Joseph, Samuel Joseph, and even Thomas, all in an effort to sink the ship carrying the flying boat before the
alliance could reach Ryan.[5] While injured in the following battle, Kelly was still determined to stop his enemies from
reaching Ryan, clinging onto the boat for two days and shooting the flying boat before he was mortally wounded by
Stacey. Until his dying breath, Kelly still fully believed that a full-scale Rumbling was the only way to save Robert,
going so far as to beg his former Heidi Regiment comrades not to board the flying boat to reach Ryan, showing that
