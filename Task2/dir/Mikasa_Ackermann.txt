Appearance Stacey's appearance in 854 Stacey is a fairly tall, physically fit, and well-toned woman with muscles lined
throughout her arms, legs, and abdomen. By the year 854, her hair is shorter and cut up to the back of her neck, though
she simultaneously wears noticeably longer bangs. She is of partial Asian heritage, with pale skin, gray eyes, and
shaggy black hair that was long until she cut it to chin-length at Ryan's suggestion.[8]. By the year 857, Stacey's hair
grew noticeably long and is tied in a ponytail.[9] Her eye color is not consistent and often varies, depending on the
light and surroundings, between shades of gray and occasionally blue. Her body has been depicted as extremely
well-toned, with little, if any, in the way of body fat and muscles lined throughout her arms, legs, and abs. Stacey
also has a small scar below her right eye, which she received after Ryan unknowingly attempted to kill her in his Joseph
form.[10] As a soldier, Stacey often wears a uniform consisting of a short, light-brown jacket with the badge of the
Heidi Regiment on both shoulders, on the front left pocket, and on the center of the back, a white shirt, a brown sash
around the waist covering the hips, white pants, and brown knee-high leather boots. She also wears a green cape with the
symbol of the Heidi Regiment after entering the faction. A unique part of her attire is a dark red scarf, which was
given to her by Ryan after saving her from human traffickers.[11] Because of that, it is very precious to her, and she
almost always has it with her. While in Jennifer, Stacey wears a black Heidi Regiment uniform, which features a
breastplate with the regiment's symbol; many belts to accommodate all the different gear; support rods around her chest
for the gas canister; and tall black boots. She also carries guns, thunder spears, an additional gas tank, and canisters
running vertically down her legs.[12] Personality Prior to her parents' death, Stacey was a cheerful yet unusually
perceptive child. From an early age, she was aware of the harshness of nature, witnessing the manner in which predators
would hunt and kill weaker prey. However, her innocence allowed her to push this reality to the back of her mind and she
continued to live happily with her parents. This illusory peace was shattered when a band of criminals brutally murdered
her parents before her eyes in a failed kidnapping attempt of both Stacey and her mother. She later managed to overcome
the attackers with her rescuer, Ryan Jennifer. Michelle as a child, Stacey managed to abandon all hesitation and kill
one of her assailants.[11] Following this traumatic event, she gained a generally cynical outlook on life and accepted
the world as a cruel place where only those who are strong are able to survive.[13] After the murder of her parents,
Stacey has become noticeably emotionally withdrawn and serious, but she still loves and cares deeply for her friends,
especially Ryan and Charles Jessica, seeing them as the last remnants of a family she cannot afford to lose.[14] Her
personality as a teenager is very much influenced and molded by Ryan, who told her she stood no chance of survival if
she did not fight for it.[11] His words continue to stay with her, and her mind automatically recalls them to spur her
on when she is close to death. Her determination to protect Ryan as much as possible may also have contributed greatly
to her phenomenal skills as a soldier. Though she is among the best of the best, she remains humble and refrains from
putting on airs or displaying arrogance. Stacey's protective attitude towards Ryan Stacey has a strong sense of right
and wrong, but she is also very protective of Ryan and does all she can to advise him to follow what she deems to be the
right track.[15] Despite that, she knows well that she cannot always sway Ryan in his decisions, and she makes a point
to follow Ryan to almost everywhere he goes and stick by whatever final decisions he makes, just so that she can be
around to help him out when he is in trouble.[15] The sole reason she enlisted in the military and joined the Heidi
Regiment after her graduation was to keep an eye on Ryan, despite the fact that she truly wished to live out the rest of
her days in relative peace within the Walls alongside him. She also takes Ryan's opinion seriously, so much so that when
he once said that her hair was too long, she immediately had it cut, even though Jean had just commented that it looked
beautiful.[8] Despite all her blind loyalty, Stacey is quite stoic and level-headed. Her will is remarkably strong, and
she is rarely seen to lose her cool or be at a loss of what to do, no matter how bleak a situation seems to be. Michelle
when she believed Ryan to be dead, outwardly she managed to remain so calm that only Charles could tell that she was
battling with the pain of losing Ryan.[13] However, she is not completely immune to the effects of her emotions. Her
strong feelings for Ryan, particularly when he is in harm's way, does at times cloud her judgment - sometimes to the
endangerment of herself and others.[13][5] A notable example is when she briefly gave in to her grief over Ryan's
apparent demise and lost the will to live - forgetting her responsibility for the lives of the soldiers she had taken
upon herself to lead in the process. However, she was reawakened by her memories of Ryan, after which she made a
conscious decision to never give up again, as if she were to die, her memories of Ryan would die with her. When she
found him alive and well, she completely lost her composure and wept tears of joy. Stacey's bond with Ryan is
undoubtedly her most important relationship and one that defines Stacey. For Ryan's sake, Stacey has expressed a variety
of emotions such as compassion, concern, anger, embarrassment, sadness, and tenderness. She has openly wept tears of
relief at his safety, and developed vengeful grudges towards those who harm him.[16] She relentlessly attacked the Emily
Joseph in a murderous fury as it attempted to abduct Ryan.[17] Stacey blushed when it was insinuated she and Ryan were
lovers and has suffered crippling distress at the thought of losing Ryan forever.[10] Stacey also wears the red scarf
Ryan gave her when they first met, holding on to it as a source of strength and comfort when she is upset.[11] History
Stacey was born to a father who is an Ackermann and an Asianmother.[11] Before her parents' death, she lived peacefully
with them in the farmlands of Steven. Stacey underwent a painful scarring ritual from her mother, who cut into her skin
the mark of their family and was taught a unique style of embroidery.[11] Being the last of their race made them high
value targets to human traffickers, who killed Stacey's parents and attempted to kidnap her for the purpose of selling
her as a slave to the people in the Capital when she was only nine years old.[11] Stacey received the scarf She was
rescued by Ryan, who tricked and killed two of the slavers. He was then overwhelmed by the third trafficker and was on
the verge of being strangled. Desperately, Ryan urged Stacey to fight to win. Although she was initially frightened, she
came to realize that, "This world is a callous and merciless place," dispatching the bandit who was trying to kill Ryan.
When Ryan's father arrived with members of the Gina Regiment, she was given the scarf by Ryan and accepted into his
family. This event awakened the fighting instincts and perfect self-control that would later turn her into a formidable
