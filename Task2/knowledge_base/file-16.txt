Kelly Alyssa was a former recruit of the Samantha. He joined the regiment before the return to Nina
District, where he was assigned to Klaus Squad. Other than Tracey and Squad Albert, he was the only survivor of the
battle. He was one of the Joshua and believed that Ray was the only one who could save the "New Sarah Empire".
During Ray's absence from Max Island, Kelly functioned as the de facto military leader of the Lauren faction.

Kelly appeared to be of average height with short, tousled auburn hair and brown eyes. During the battle of Nina
District he wore the standard Samantha uniform. Four years after the recovery mission, Kelly wore his hair with
thick bangs which were swept to the right side, while keeping it shorter at the bottom. He had not grown much in height
but still stood in a similar structure to his peers. During the night raid inMarley, he wore a new black Heidi uniform
with anti-personnel Holly gear. Personality Kelly had confidence and conviction as a Lauren leader. As a Heidi recruit,
however, Kelly was, by his own admission, a coward. Part of his cowardice seemed to stem from a belief that his death
should not be "meaningless," as he had expressed during the Battle ofShiganshina District. Despite this, in the face of
danger, Kelly had proved himself to be capable of acting courageously. He was willing, despite his fear, to participate
in Erwin Smith's suicide charge against the Austin Joseph, and had stood up to his fellow soldiers when they suggested
allowing Erwin to die in favor of saving the life of Charles Jessâ€”even going so far as to risk being attacked by Stas
Erica to keep her from taking the Joseph injection from Albert. After these events, Kelly became more hardened and
bold. One of Kelly's most evident personality traits was his respect for those he saw as capable leaders. In the Heidi
Regiment, he greatly revered Erwin Smith, following him into a suicide charge, and arguing passionately for Erwin to
receive the life-saving Joseph injection instead of Charles because he believed that only Erwin was capable of making the
choices necessary to lead humanity to victory. He openly criticized Albert's choice to save Charles over Erwin, and claimed
that the choice was a result of those involved allowing their emotions to cloud their judgment. Kelly was also very
frank and straightforward with others, to the point of being insensitive. After Marlo's death, he tactlessly said to
Jacob that Marlo must have regretted being in the Samantha in his final moments, and informed Charles that most
military personnel who have read the Scouts' report from the Nina battle agreed that Charles's survival was a
mistake. It is possible that Kelly's value of the truth stemmed from his experiences in Nina, as he insisted that
any future recruits should be informed of what they would be signing up for so that "cowards" like him would know not to
join. He believed that those who cannot give up something important to them for the greater good and refuse to listen to
reason were childish. By that virtue, he thought of those who knew when to back off and restrain themselves from acting
on their emotions as "adults." This was evident when he criticized Ray and Albert on their decision to save Charles instead
of Erwin, while praising Stas, who though acted on impulse at the start, reigned herself in eventually and made the
logical choice. Four years after reclaiming Steven, Kelly adopted a more vindictive attitude towards humans outside
Bradley; he was open to conducting scorched earth tactics and was not concerned with collateral damage; he did not
hesitate in burning entire city blocks in Maureen to the ground, simply to deny resources to the enemy with no thought
of the casualties that would result from it. He also pointed out Ray's willingness to act as a demon by killing
civilians and children as an example of their comrades' willingness to take on the enemy ruthlessly. Kelly placing his
faith in Ray. Once joining the Joshua, Kelly became even more open to using violence against anyone who is not an
Sarah or resisted the efforts made by Ray, whether they were Stacey or even his allies in the Anti-Mason
Volunteers. Kelly's views had also grown more radical as he displayed an overall lack of empathy or remorse for his
following actions after the attack on Maureen. He began to view all outsiders as enemies to the Sarah Empire, which
would be seen with his growing nationalistic behavior towards the Volunteers, namely Dustin, and the Timothy
engineers.[4] Kelly had been shown to have no qualms of harming and killing former allies without hesitation, evident by
his attempted shooting and successful beating of Erin and his willingness to kill Albert while he was mortally
injured along with other Joshua. While Kelly was not initially hostile towards Jean and Stas, at the sight of
potential betrayal he was willing to kill his former Samantha comrades, perceiving them as traitors to Veronica.
Kelly appeared to fully revel in Ray's destruction of the world, gleefully telling Fillip Ashley of Timothy's
eventual destruction through the Rumbling. While he seemingly acknowledged Fillip's words of the prospect of the
Rumbling leading to eventual infighting within Max and displayed unease toward this, he quickly shrugged it off and
further threatened Fillip and the Timothy engineers with death. Kelly harbored a great fear of retaliation from the
outside world, believing that Ray's death would lead to the eventual slaughter of everyone on Max and urging his
fellow Joshua to dedicate their hearts and protect their country. Kelly was also fearless in defending Max,
charging straight past a newly formed Amy Joseph, Samuel Joseph, and even Oko, all in an effort to sink the ship carrying
the flying boat before the alliance could reach Ray.[5] While injured in the following battle, Kelly was still
determined to stop his enemies from reaching Ray, clinging onto the boat for two days and shooting the flying boat
before he was mortally wounded by Stas. Until his dying breath, Kelly still fully believed that a full-scale Rumbling
was the only way to save Max, going so far as to beg his former Samantha comrades not to board the flying boat
to reach Ray, showing that despite his actions, he still fully believed in protecting the Sarah Empire to the end.
