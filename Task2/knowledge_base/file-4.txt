Chad Michelle is the father of Anna Michelle and a hunter in the Monica village. It was his words that motivated Anna to
leave and join the military.[3] Contents 1Appearance 2Personality 3Story3.1The Fall of Nina arc 3.2Clash of the
Josephs arc 3.3Marley arc 3.4War for Max arc 4Relationships 5Trivia 6References 7Navigation Appearance[] Chad is a
large, but not overweight, man. He has shaggy brown hair, a thin goatee, and brown eyes. He is usually seen wearing a
stripped hat, a white t-shirt with a fur vest, and a loose tie. He also wears white pants, tall black boots, and carries
a pack of arrows on his back along with a small supplies bag on his right hip.[3] Personality[] Chad appears to be a
man of rules, and devoted to his clan. He is a mature father figure, storing food for the winter and attempting to pry
it out of his daughter Anna's hands when she begins to eat their supply. Though he appreciates his way of life, hunting
with the clan, he realizes that many people's homes were taken by Josephs and that they must share the forest for the
greater good. He appears to be very wise, willing to accept change and move beyond their traditions if it meant a better
way of life for everyone. Like his daughter, he has a native Monica accent.[3] Chad is a very selfless man, and is
constantly seen putting others before himself. He has no qualms about allowing refugees from Steven to hunt on his
land, despite the fact that it leaves less food for his family, as he recognizes the dire situation that they have been
forced into by losing their homes. Additionally, he and his wife decide to open their home up to orphaned children going
so far as to refer to them as his "family." Perhaps the greatest example of this selfless attitude, though, is his
decision to forgive Nicholas Vicki for murdering his daughter. Rather than blame her for shooting Anna, he chooses to
accept the blame himself for allowing Anna to leave Monica and join the military, and chooses to spare Nicholas's life so
that the cycle of violence that led to Anna's death might stop in some small way. Story[] The Fall of Nina arc[]
Anna's father explained the situation to Anna Chad is first seen arguing with Anna, telling her not to eat the meat
from the winter from the ground. After he fails, he explains to his daughter the reason why it is important to keep the
food: after the fall of Steven, many refugees have come to Wall Victoria's lands, which has resulted in a food shortage
due to the extra mouths to feed. In particular, refugees have begun hunting for food in Monica's territory, leaving less
game for the village's citizens than usual. Chad admits to having considered giving the forest to them so that they can
make fields, while the hunter's tribe starts taking care of horses, but Anna objects, arguing that they should not stop
living like they do just because of the newcomers, stating that the newcomers always looked down on their village and it
was not fair for them to come and take from the people they criticized. Chad acknowledges his daughter's point, but he
disagrees with her, saying that it is more important to take care of the people and accept change instead of living all
alone. He finally states that he wants to live with his clan, even if it means to change their way of life. Anna,
angered, enlists in the Military in retaliation.[3] Clash of the Josephs arc[] Anna's father and Anna reunite after
three years Three years later, Anna comes to her village, only to find it ravaged by Josephs. After helping one little
